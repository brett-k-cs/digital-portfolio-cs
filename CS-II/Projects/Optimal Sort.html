Explanation of Graph:

The graph shows the average execution time of the sorting algorithms in relation to the amount of words it was tested on. It proves that the order of the strings, the number of the strings, and the type of algorithm all affect the total execution time. With optimal conditions (a sorted list), all three algorithms took the least amount of time compared to the worst conditions and random conditions. Selection sort had the least difference though because of how it functions - it still checks more words than the other algorithms. The number of strings exponentially affects the time it takes to sort. The different algorithms have differing times because they have different internal components. Selection sort goes through all elements after the current one it checks.
